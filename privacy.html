PRIVACY POLICY

Last updated April 29, 2026



This Privacy Notice for Candid Finance Ltd (doing business as Candid Finance) ('we', 'us', or 'our'), describes how and why we might access, collect, store, use, and/or share ('process') your personal information when you use our services ('Services'), including when you:
  • Visit our website at https://candid-personal-finance-test1.vercel.app or any website of ours that links to this Privacy Notice
  • Download and use our mobile application (Candid Finance), or any other application of ours that links to this Privacy Notice
  • Use Candid Finance. Candid is a free UK personal finance guidance tool that helps working professionals understand and optimise their financial position. Users input financial details including salary, pension contributions, savings, investments, and debt balances. Candid analyses these inputs to generate a personalised financial health score, identify specific opportunities for improvement, and provide actionable guidance across eight areas: cash and savings, investments and capital gains tax, pensions, student loans, mortgages, insurance, family finances, and inheritance planning. Candid does not provide regulated financial advice. All outputs are educational guidance based on user-provided data. The tool uses artificial intelligence to generate personalised commentary and recommendations. Anonymised user data is stored to improve the accuracy and relevance of the tool's outputs.
  • Engage with us in other related ways, including any marketing or events
Questions or concerns? Reading this Privacy Notice will help you understand your privacy rights and choices. We are responsible for making decisions about how your personal information is processed. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our Services. If you still have any questions or concerns, please contact us at contact@candidpersonalfinance.co.uk.

11. CONTROLS FOR DO-NOT-TRACK FEATURES


Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems and mobile applications include a Do-Not-Track ('DNT') feature or setting you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected. At this stage no uniform technology standard for recognising and implementing DNT signals has been finalised. As such, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online. If a standard for online tracking is adopted that we must follow in the future, we will inform you about that practice in a revised version of this Privacy Notice.
